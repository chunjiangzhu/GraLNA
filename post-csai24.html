REU Site: Graph Learning and Network Analysis: from Foundations to Applications
May 27-July 18, 2025, Greensboro, NC

Paper Accepted in the 2024 8th International Conference on Computer Science and Artificial Intelligence

A research paper entitled "Stochastic Variance-Reduced Iterative Hard Thresholding in Graph Sparsity Optimization" was accepted and to appear in the 2024 8th International Conference on Computer Science and Artificial Intelligence. Congratulations, Derek and Samuel!

Abstract: Stochastic optimization algorithms are widely used for large-scale data analysis due to their low per-iteration costs, but they often suffer from slow asymptotic convergence caused by inherent variance. Variance-reduced techniques have been therefore used to address this issue in structured sparse models utilizing sparsity-inducing norms or â„“0-norms. However, these techniques are not directly applicable to complex (non-convex) graph sparsity models, which are essential in applications like disease outbreak monitoring and social network analysis. In this paper, we introduce two stochastic variance-reduced gradient-based methods to solve graph sparsity optimization: GraphSVRG-IHT and GraphSCSG-IHT. We provide a general framework for theoretical analysis, demonstrating that our methods enjoy a linear convergence speed. Extensive experiments validate the efficiency and effectiveness of our proposed algorithms.
